# Contributor License Agreement — AsymmFlow

*Plain-English summary first; the operative terms follow. This is not legal advice.*

## Why this exists (the 30-second version)

AsymmFlow makes a public promise in [`LICENSE-ROADMAP.md`](LICENSE-ROADMAP.md):
every release becomes **MIT** two years after it ships. For that promise to be real,
the maintainers must have the right to relicense **all** the code — including yours.
If even one contributor's lines couldn't be relicensed, the whole MIT-flip would
break for everyone.

So this CLA does the minimum needed to keep that promise alive. It does **not** take
your copyright away — you keep owning your work. It just lets us include your
contribution in the same AGPL-today → MIT-in-two-years journey as everything else.

## What you agree to by contributing

By submitting a contribution (a pull request, patch, or any code/docs/assets) to this
project, you agree that:

1. **You wrote it / you have the right to submit it.** The contribution is your
   original work, or you otherwise have the right to submit it under these terms, and
   you're not knowingly violating anyone else's rights.

2. **You keep your copyright.** You retain all ownership of your contribution. This is
   a *license* grant, not an assignment.

3. **Inbound license (now).** You license your contribution to the project and to
   everyone who receives the project under the **GNU AGPL-3.0** — the project's
   current license.

4. **Rolling relicense to MIT (the roadmap).** You agree your contribution follows the
   same schedule as the rest of the project: each release containing your contribution
   becomes available under the **MIT License** two years after that release ships, as
   described in [`LICENSE-ROADMAP.md`](LICENSE-ROADMAP.md).

5. **Maintainer relicensing right.** You grant the AsymmFlow maintainers a perpetual,
   worldwide, non-exclusive, royalty-free, irrevocable license to use, reproduce,
   modify, distribute, and **sublicense** your contribution, and to **offer it under
   other license terms** (including more permissive licenses such as MIT, and
   including license terms granted to specific recipients). This is what makes both
   the MIT-flip and any future "MIT it for me early" arrangement possible. We will
   never use this right to *restrict* rights already granted to the public — we only
   ever loosen.

6. **No warranty / no obligation.** You provide your contribution "as is," with no
   warranties, and the maintainers are under no obligation to accept or merge it.

That's the whole deal. It exists so the commons can count on the two-year clock.

## How to sign

We keep this lightweight — no separate forms to mail. **Sign off each commit** with
the [Developer Certificate of Origin](https://developercertificate.org/) trailer,
which records your agreement to the above:

```
git commit -s -m "your message"
```

This appends a line like:

```
Signed-off-by: Your Name <your.email@example.com>
```

By signing off, you confirm you agree to this CLA for that contribution. First-time
contributors: please also say "I agree to the CLA" in your first pull request so the
agreement is explicit on the record.
